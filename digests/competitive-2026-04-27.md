## Competitor Developments

### Trace — ACTIVE

**What:** Trace has been executing an aggressive state-association blitz throughout Q1/Q2 2026. The most recent anchor: Florida Youth Soccer Association (FYSA) named Trace its official video partner on **April 8**, offering every FYSA-sanctioned team one free sideline camera + one PlayerFocus™ Pro subscription (promo code `FYSAFREE`). This follows deals with New Jersey Youth Soccer (March 2026), Eastern Pennsylvania Youth Soccer (March 2026), Georgia Soccer (February 2026), Iowa Soccer (February 2026), Indiana Soccer, Washington Youth Soccer, Ohio Soccer, and Hawaii YSA — roughly 9–10 state associations in under 12 weeks.

**PlayerFocus Pro** automatically identifies, follows, and spotlights individual players during a match, delivering a personalized highlight reel per player. The Pro tier sits above the free camera offering and is the upsell vector.

**Source:** [FYSA announcement](https://www.fysa.com/news/2026/04/08/florida-youth-soccer-and-trace-launch-free-camera-initative/) · [EPYSA announcement](https://www.epysa.org/news/2026/03/06/eastern-pennsylvania-youth-soccer-partners-with-trace-to-bring-advanced-video-tools-to-players-and-coaches/) · [Georgia Soccer](https://www.georgiasoccer.org/news/2026/02/13/trace-partnership/) · [Iowa Soccer](https://www.iowasoccer.org/news/2026/02/06/trace-partnership/) · [NJYS](https://www.njyouthsoccer.com/news/trace-partnership/)

**Why it matters for composed:** Trace is commoditizing video capture at the state-association level through free hardware, which normalizes video-for-every-player expectations — but their product stops at highlights; they don't offer structured training programs, IDP goals, or the player-development layer that composed owns.

## Synthesis

### Threat Radar — Top 3 This Week

1. **Trace's state-association lock-in** is the most structurally dangerous move in the space right now. By distributing free cameras through official governing bodies, Trace is becoming the default infrastructure for youth soccer video across states where composed wants to grow. Once a team has a Trace camera and PlayerFocus Pro subscription, the mental switching cost for any additional software tool goes up.

2. **Veo Coach Assist expanding AI from camera to coaching language** blurs the line between recording platform and development tool. Veo already has the camera installed at thousands of clubs; adding AI coaching language on top means they could organically capture the same "post-session insight" use case that composed is building around. The Base plan expansion makes this more accessible to price-sensitive youth clubs.

3. **Hudl's "get scouted" narrative around youth video** (Vertex Cup, Wyscout pipeline) taps directly into the aspirational motivation of U14–U18 players. Composed currently motivates players through XP, badges, and streaks; if players come to believe that video on Wyscout is the real career lever, Hudl/Veo platforms gain a status value that gamification alone can't counter.

---

### Opportunity Radar — Top 3 This Week

1. **No competitor owns the player-first mobile training loop.** Trace and Veo are fundamentally camera/team tools; the player logs into them (if at all) to watch their clip. Composed's guided live sessions, drill library, IDP goals, and XP system are the only product in this set built around daily player self-improvement habits. This differentiation is unclaimed territory.

2. **Parent visibility is a white space.** None of the named competitors offer a parent-facing dashboard. With ~45 states still unpartnered with Trace, there is a clear opening to approach clubs by leading with the parent connection as a family-engagement feature — a value prop that state associations and club directors find compelling because it reduces parent-coach friction.

3. **Hardware-free onboarding is a meaningful edge in price-sensitive markets.** Trace's free camera model is only free via state-association codes; direct teams pay ~$495 for hardware. Veo Cam 3 is $999+. Composed works on any phone a player already owns. As 2026 World Cup interest spikes participation at grassroots/under-resourced clubs, the zero-hardware-cost message could open doors in urban and lower-income markets where Trace and Veo have minimal penetration.

---

### One Concrete Move

**In the next 7 days: draft and send direct outreach to 3–5 state youth soccer associations (prioritizing states *not yet* in Trace's network) positioning composed as the player-development layer.**

Framing: *"Your teams can now record and review video — Trace/Veo handles that. Composed gives those same players a place to set goals, follow structured training programs, and track their improvement week over week. No additional hardware required."*

This is the moment to move on this before Trace signs another 5–6 state deals and the remaining unpartnered associations feel like less valuable targets. The pitch isn't anti-Trace — it's additive — which makes it an easier yes for a state association that already made a video deal and is looking for ways to increase the program's perceived value to member clubs and families.
